Group Processes

The team approached this project following software development practices commonly used in the industry to manage the development of Plutus. This included utilizing an Agile approach, which emphasizes flexibility and responsiveness to change (Highsmith 2002). The team held regular meetings, such as stand-up meetings, to keep everyone informed about the progress and to identify and resolve any issues that arose. They also used iterations to deliver working versions of the software incrementally, allowing for early testing and feedback from stakeholders. Additionally, the team used a version control system to manage the source code, and they conducted regular code reviews to ensure the code met the team's standards. The team also practiced continuous integration and continuous deployment (CI/CD) to ensure that the software was always in a releasable state (Fowler 2018). This helped the team to catch and fix errors early in the development process, reducing the risk of costly delays or bugs in the final product. Overall, the team's goal was to mimic the software development process practices commonly used in the industry to ensure the development of a high-quality product.
